My dear juniors, it truly pains me to see you all wasting your precious college years. Let me offer you a piece of advice: get out of those traps filled with laughter and joy, and return to the 'right path.'

First, move out of the dorms immediately.
What is there to miss about dorm life? Staying up late eating midnight snacks, sharing secrets, or gaming with roommates—aside from gaining lifelong friendships and learning how to tolerate others, what does that add to your resume? Will an interviewer hire you because you’re 'great at bonding over midnight snacks'? Of course not. Withdraw from the dorms now, and trade that social time for memorizing textbooks in the library. That is what I call being 'down-to-earth.'

Second, avoid extracurricular clubs at all costs.
Organizing events, practicing for performances, or pulling all-nighters for camps—aside from wasting your worthless youth and cultivating some vague, intangible 'leadership skills,' it’s a complete drain on your life. Can the applause from a stage be exchanged for cash? Don't be naive. Rather than shining on stage, you’d be better off rotting in the corner of a library. Remember, your textbooks have more 'soul' than any sports competition or dance performance.

The most dangerous trap of all is falling in love in college.
Relationships are nothing but stumbling blocks to your life plan. Walking hand-in-hand on campus, getting through low points together, learning emotional maturity—does any of this help you get a 6.5 on your IELTS? Can love help you secure a referral to a Fortune 500 company? It cannot. No matter how sweet a relationship is, it cannot compare to a slight smile from your future boss. Listen to me: use that time to drill past exam papers. If you really want to experience the sensation of your 'heart racing,' just take a look at your lagging GPA or your unsubmitted internship applications; I guarantee you’ll feel the surge instantly.

Finally, vacations are for resting? Stop kidding yourself.
While others are traveling, experiencing the world, and enriching their souls, you need to wake up. You should be seamlessly slotting yourself into any company willing to exploit you. Intern, intern, and intern again. You don’t need a life; you only need work experience.

Remember, you only have one goal in college: to land a high-paying job by the time you're 22. A clean, polished resume is far more 'down-to-earth' than a colorful life filled with memories and tears, don't you agree?
