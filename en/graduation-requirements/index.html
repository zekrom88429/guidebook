    不要像我一延畢 Don't get dalyed in graduation like me

    "Graduation requirements" might sound like something you only need to worry about during your senior year—after all, four years is a long time. However, at Lingnan University, some of these requirements must be accumulated starting from your very first year. You won’t officially cross the finish line until they are fully completed right before graduation!

    To avoid facing delayed graduation (like I did), make sure to save this list and start planning your university life early.

    Lingnan University Graduation Requirements at a Glance

    To graduate from the university, you must fulfill five major requirements, covering both academic performance and co-curricular development:

    1. cGPA Requirement
    2. ILP (Integrated Learning Programme) Hours
    3. English Language Graduation Requirements (ELGR)
    4. Service Learning (SL)
    5. Information Technology Fluency Programme (ITFP) Test

    cGPA Requirement: The Academic Baseline

    The most fundamental condition for graduation is that you must pass all compulsory courses and maintain a cumulative GPA (cGPA) of 1.67 or above.

    Frequently skipping classes and failing to submit assignments can genuinely prevent you from graduating on time. Do not take this lightly! I know people who had to graduate a semester late simply because of poor class attendance.

    How is cGPA Calculated?

    cGPA is the standard used to measure a student's academic performance. It is calculated by multiplying the credits of each course by the Grade Point earned, which gives the "Weighted Points." Finally, the total weighted points are divided by the total number of credits.

    【Calculation Example】 Suppose you take 5 courses this semester, each worth 3 credits (15 credits in total):

    Course Grade Grade Point Credits Weighted Points (Grade Point × Credits)
    Course A A 4.0 3 12.0
    Course B B+ 3.3 3 9.9
    Course C B 3.0 3 9.0
    Course D C+ 2.3 3 6.9
    Course E B- 2.7 3 8.1
    Total - - 15 45.9
    • Total Weighted Points: 45.9
    • Total Credits: 15
    • Your cGPA: 45.9 / 15 = 3.06

    English Language Graduation Requirements (ELGR)

    All students must fulfill the language requirement by either passing the Password Test or achieving the required IELTS score. This requirement can be quite challenging, so it is highly recommended to allocate sufficient time to prepare for it.

    Comparison of the Two Assessment Methods

    Assessment Attempt Requirements Graduation Score Threshold Fees & Resources
    Password Test
    (Easier, but has limited external recognition)
    • 4-Year Undergraduate Students: Take it twice in Year 1 and twice in Year 3–4 (4 times total).
    • Direct Entry / Senior-year Intake: Take it once in Year 3 and once in Year 4 (2 times total).
    • Most Majors: 6.5
    • Department of Chinese: 6.0
    • Department of English / Translation: 7.0
    		 Arranged and funded by the university.
    IELTS
    (Globally recognized; can be taken anytime during your studies)    		 All 4-year and Senior-year Intake students.
    • Most Majors: 6.5
    • Department of Chinese: 6.0
    • Department of English / Translation: 7.0
    • On-campus computer-based test fee is waived.
    • Off-campus test registration fees can be subsidized.
    • Official Preparation Resources: The university provides Online IELTS Resources for candidates. You can also make full use of the support services offered by the ELSS (English Language Support Service) to boost your English proficiency.

    Service Learning (SL)

    Before graduating, you must enroll in and pass at least one Service Learning (SL) course. Unlike traditional lecture-based classes, these courses emphasize hands-on experience, where students independently plan and execute community service projects under the guidance of a supervisor.

    • How to Enroll: You register for it just like a regular course during the Course Registration period. The Registry's website provides a list of approved Service Learning courses available each term.
    • How to Pass: Complete the designated service activities according to the course guidelines and submit your reports on time.

    Because Service Learning requires high autonomy and involves a relatively heavy workload and challenge, it is highly recommended to take it during a semester when your academic workload is lighter.

    ILP Hours: Enriching Your Co-curricular Life

    The ILP (Integrated Learning Programme) comprises out-of-class holistic learning activities provided by the university. For every hour of participation, you earn 1 ILP point.

    The 6 ILP Categories

    • CELD: Civic Education and Leadership Development
    • IED: Intellectual and Entrepreneurship Development
    • SEW: Social and Emotional Well-being
    • PFW: Physical Fitness and Well-being
    • AD: Aesthetic Development
    • RE: Residential Education

    Requirements for Freshmen vs. Senior-year Intakes

    • 4-Year Undergraduate Freshmen:
      • Core Requirements: You must earn at least 5 points in each of the six categories (30 points total).
      • Free Electives: You must accumulate an additional 30 points from any category of your choice.
      • Total baseline requirement: 60 points.
    • Direct Entry / Senior-year Intake Students:
      • You only need to meet the core requirement of 3 points per category (18 points total). You are exempt from the 30 free elective points.

    Adjustments

    • Absence from NSO: If you miss the New Student Orientation without a valid reason, your graduation requirement will increase by an extra 15 points of Free Electives.
    • Exchange Programmes: Successfully completing a university Exchange Programme grants you 7.5 points toward your Free Electives.

    How to Record Your Attendance and Points?

    • In-person Activities: A QR code will be displayed at the beginning and end of the event. Make sure to scan it with your phone to check in and out.
    • Online Activities: Conducted via Zoom; the system will automatically log your attendance duration.
    • The Crucial Step: Within a week after the event ends, you will receive an Evaluation Form and a Reflection Report in your university email. Your ILP points will ONLY be officially credited to your account after you complete and submit both forms!

    How to Register for Activities & Earn ILP Points?

    The university mainly distributes ILP activity information opportunities through three main channels:

    Channel Description & Key Takeaways
    1. Online Central Registration At the beginning of each semester, the university holds a centralized registration period. You will need to log into the system and select your preferred ILP activities, just like course registration.
    Note: Quotas are limited and offered on a first-come, first-served basis, so be sure to act fast!
    2. Email Notifications Various university departments and organizations will send event invitations via email from time to time. Make sure to check your university inbox regularly!
    3. Joining University Teams If you become an official team member and maintain an attendance rate of 80% or above, you can earn a substantial amount of ILP points directly:
    • Sports Teams: Awarded 12 PFW points directly (Physical Fitness and Well-being).
    • Cultural Teams: Awarded 12 AD points directly (Aesthetic Development).

    Information Technology Fluency Programme (ITFP)

    Before graduation, you must pass the online ITFP test provided by the university.

    • Test Content: Covers basic computer operations and practical general knowledge of information literacy.
    • Test Link: Access the online ITFP Platform.

    💡 A word from a senior: This test is a simple pass/fail assessment and is not difficult at all. Since you can take it at any point during your four years, I highly recommend getting it out of the way during your freshman year so you don't find yourself rushing to do it right before graduation.

    Wishing you all a wonderful, fulfilling, and exciting four years at university. Graduate on time, and don't follow in my footsteps!
